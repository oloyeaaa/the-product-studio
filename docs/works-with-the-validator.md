# Works with The Validator

The Product Studio is one system in a line of installable employees. This page is honest about where it
starts, what feeds it, and how the two systems compose when you own both.

## The line

- **The Validator (System 01)** owns the front of the journey: it reads real demand in your niche, ranks
  ideas against evidence, calls the verdict, and hands you a pre-sell test. It ends with a validated idea.
- **The Product Studio (System 02)** owns the middle: it takes that idea and builds the finished product,
  the cover, the sales page, and the checkout checklist. It ends with a listing you can sell.

Each stops where the other starts, on purpose. A researcher that also built the thing would be marking its
own homework, and a builder that also validated demand would always say yes to itself.

## The handoff is automatic

There is nothing to configure and nothing to export. When both systems are installed on the same machine,
The Product Studio looks for The Validator's memory at build time. If validated (or testing) ideas exist,
it lists them and offers to build straight from one. Say yes and the idea, the buyer, the pain, the
promise, and the price band all carry over from the validated row into the build.

If The Validator isn't installed, or you'd rather not use it for this build, a short brief captures the
same shape: what it is, who it's for, the pain, the promise, and a price. Nothing breaks. Each system
stands completely alone.

## What a validated idea gives a build that a cold brief cannot

A brief tells the build what you believe. A validated row tells it what the market said:

- **An evidence-backed promise.** The promise on a validated row survived a demand read and a real
  pre-sell test. The build puts it straight into the product and the sales page headline, knowing buyers
  already raised their hands for it.
- **A price the market supported.** The price band was justified against what comparable products actually
  sell for, not guessed. The build and the sales page carry it as-is.
- **The pain in the buyer's own words.** The sales page's pain section is written from phrases real buyers
  used, not from your best guess at what they'd say.
- **Proof there's a buyer at all.** The most expensive product mistake is building something nobody asked
  for. A validated idea has already cleared that bar; a cold brief clears it only after you've built.

## Which to start with

If you already know exactly what to build and who buys it, start here: brief in, product out. If you have
ideas but no evidence, start with The Validator, let it kill the weak ones cheaply, and bring the survivor
here. Both roads end at the same place: a finished product with a sales page and a working checkout.
